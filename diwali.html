Diwali - The Festival of Lights

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most important festivals in Hinduism. It is celebrated every year in the months of October or November, depending on the Hindu calendar. The festival signifies the triumph of good over evil, light over darkness, and knowledge over ignorance.

This festival is celebrated because Lord Rama came back from 14 years of exile. The festival is celebrated by lighting diyas or candles in and around homes, and bursting firecrackers. People decorate their homes with rangolis, flowers, and lights, and exchange sweets and gifts with their loved ones. It is also a time for families to come together, prepare traditional foods, and offer prayers to deities such as Lord Ganesha and Goddess Lakshmi.

Diwali is not just celebrated in India, but also in other countries such as Nepal, Sri Lanka, Malaysia, and Singapore. It is a time of joy and happiness, and brings people of all cultures and religions together.
